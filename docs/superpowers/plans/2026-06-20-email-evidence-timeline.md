## Cross-Cutting Patterns

### PDPC's Four Shifting Justifications
1. **7 May 2024:** "Not your personal data" / "firm grounds required beyond suspicion"
2. **10 May 2024:** "Section 21 is not for discovery / civil proceedings"
3. **20 Jun 2024:** Fifth Schedule §1(f) and (h) — police investigation exceptions
4. **14 Aug 2024:** Section 4(2) — data intermediary shield

None of these were cited by the MCSTs in their original refusals. Each was introduced by PDPC on the MCSTs' behalf. Each time the Complainant addressed one, PDPC introduced a new one and ceased communication.

### The Deletion Timeline Gap
- 17 Apr: Verbal access request → s.22A preservation duty triggered
- 25 Apr: Written request → preservation duty reinforced
- 29 Apr: Knight Frank seeks MCST decision (within retention window)
- 30 Apr: Footage auto-deleted (claimed 17-day retention)
- 2 May: Formal refusal letter (cites "privacy," not a Fifth Schedule ground)
- 4 May: Complaint to PDPC
- Aug 2024: PDPC opens investigation (~3 months after deletion)

### The Escalation Loop
Every oversight body either bounced to another or deferred back to PDPC/IMDA:
PDPC → IMDA QSM (7-month silence) → PSD (compelled IMDA, then closed) → IMDA IAU (cleared PDPC) → PSC (deferred to IMDA) → President's Office (forwarded to "relevant agencies") → PMO (no response) → MPs (auto-replies or constituency mismatch)

### The Silence Pattern
- PDPC ceased communication after each shifting justification
- IMDA was silent for 7 months (Sep 2024–Feb 2025)
- PDPC never replied to the 23 Jun 2025 detailed legal questions
- PDPC never replied to Commissioner's personal note (23 Jul 2025)
- PDPC never replied to White Paper invitation (20 Aug 2025)
- PMO and SM Lee never substantively responded
- PSC closed after one-line reply

### The "Protocol" Shield
IMDA IAU's finding that PDPC "acted in accordance with its protocols" avoided engaging with whether those protocols comply with the PDPA. Protocol compliance ≠ legal correctness. This reasoning allows any agency to shield itself by claiming internal process was followed.
