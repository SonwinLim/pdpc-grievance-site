## T2-A: MDDI MOS/SMS

**Recipients (3):** jasmin-lau, rahayu-mahzam, tan-kiat-how
**Template label:** T2-A

Dear [Honorific] [Full Name],

As [Minister of State / Senior Minister of State] at MDDI, you are directly in the line of accountability for the regulator whose pattern is documented at pdpaaccessrights.sg. I am writing to flag three findings below and to ask whether your office will review PDPC's enforcement posture toward the Access Obligation.

PDPC recently redesigned its enforcement-decisions page so that it is no longer possible to filter published cases by obligation type. The previous filter exposed a striking pattern: across its entire published enforcement record, PDPC has never once found a breach of the Access Obligation. The new layout hides that pattern entirely.

To use a sporting analogy: when a referee is also the governing body, and a player scores a goal by handling the ball, the referee simply declines to look. VAR exists today, and still the referee looked away. PDPC sits in judgement of its own decisions. Nothing happens.

The numbers bear this out. While PDPC has aggressively enforced the Protection Obligation against data breaches at major operators such as Marina Bay Sands, it has never once found a breach of the Access Obligation that every Singaporean relies on to obtain their own data. The full record is at https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html.

Would your office consider issuing a public statement on what this documented pattern means for the statutory right of access that every Singaporean holds under the Act?

Yours sincerely,
