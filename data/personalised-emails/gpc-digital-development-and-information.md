Dear Members of the Government Parliamentary Committee for Digital Development and Information,

As the GPC tasked with scrutinising legislation and programmes of the Ministry of Digital Development and Information, you sit directly in the line of accountability for the regulator whose pattern is documented at pdpaaccessrights.sg. The Government Whip's office, in mooting the GPC in 1987, said its purpose was to serve as an additional channel of feedback on government policy. This email asks the GPC to exercise that function in respect of three documented findings about the Personal Data Protection Commission.

PDPC recently redesigned its enforcement-decisions page so that it is no longer possible to filter published cases by obligation type. The previous filter exposed a striking pattern: across its entire published enforcement record, PDPC has never once found a breach of the Access Obligation. The new layout hides that pattern entirely. Anyone who previously relied on the filter to assess PDPC's enforcement record can no longer do so.

To use a sporting analogy: when a referee is also the governing body, and a player scores a goal by handling the ball, the referee simply declines to look. VAR exists today, and still the referee looked away. PDPC sits in judgement of its own decisions. Nothing happens. The enforcer investigates itself and finds nothing to enforce.

Across the entirety of PDPC's published enforcement record, the Commission has pursued Protection Obligation cases aggressively, including data breach decisions against major organisations. It has never once pursued an Access Obligation breach. Marina Bay Sands attracted a significant financial penalty for data loss; the same Commission's own letter to a complainant stated that it was "aware and presently reviewing" the Access Obligation gap. The asymmetry is documented, not alleged.

I still do not know what happened to me on the night of the accident, because the footage that would have shown me was denied and then destroyed. The I rebuilt the removed filter at pdpaaccessrights.sg so others can see the pattern.

Within the GPC's remit, would the Committee consider raising this documented gap in any upcoming MDDI-related hearing, scrutiny motion, or written-question cycle?

Three sites to verify:
- https://pdpaaccessrights.sg
- https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html
- https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/organisations/regulations-decisions/enforcement-decisions?type=Commission%27s+Decisions&page=1&sort=latest
