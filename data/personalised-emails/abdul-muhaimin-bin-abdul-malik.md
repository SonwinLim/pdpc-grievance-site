Dear Mr Abdul Muhaimin Abdul Malik,

As part of the Workers' Party team representing Sengkang GRC, you work alongside colleagues who have already engaged with digital rights and data protection questions in Parliament. I write to ensure that this particular issue reaches your constituency office as well, and to ask whether your team might find it useful as a case study in what the PDPA promises in theory versus what it delivers in practice.

In March 2025, PDPC removed a filter from its published enforcement-decisions page. That filter had allowed public segmentation of cases by obligation type. After the redesign, the segmentation is gone, and the zero Access Obligation breach finding, visible in the data before the change, became inaccessible to most members of the public. The data was made inconvenient rather than deleted.

The Maradona Hand-of-God goal remains the most direct analogy. The referee was asked to review the play. He declined. The game continued. VAR exists today, and still the referee chose not to use it. The enforcer who declines to examine the evidence allows the result to stand by default.

Section 21 of the Personal Data Protection Act gives individuals the right to access their personal data. Section 24 imposes a protection obligation. PDPC has enforced s.24 actively across dozens of cases, including against major institutions. It has never published a breach finding under s.21 in 374 published enforcement decisions. The enforcement-index at https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html documents every case; the zero in the Access Obligation column is the most consistent finding in the record.

Mr Lim, I have rebuilt the filter independently at https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html so that this data remains accessible to any resident who wants to verify it. Every Singaporean deserves to know that the Access Obligation, a right written into law, has never produced a published enforcement outcome in the history of the PDPA.

Would your constituency office find it useful to have this documented gap on record, should a resident raise it with your team?

Yours sincerely,
Lim Zirui (Ray Lim)
