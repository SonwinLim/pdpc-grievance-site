Dear Yinzhou,

I hope you are well. We’ve known each other since youth, and I am writing to you as my MP and as a member of the Government backbench.

PDPC recently redesigned its enforcement-decisions website so that it is no longer possible to filter published cases by obligation type. Previously, the filter showed a striking breakdown: across PDPC's entire published enforcement record, it had never once found a breach of the Access Obligation under section 21 of the PDPA. That pattern is now hidden from public view. I rebuilt the missing filter myself, and it remains live at [https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html.](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html.) You can verify the pattern yourself by reviewing the obligation-type column.

The Maradona analogy is precise. The referee was asked to review the play. He denied every request to look at the evidence and let the goal stand. Today’s game has VAR, and still the referee looked away. The enforcer sits in judgement of its own decisions. Nothing happens.

To be specific about what zero looks like: PDPC aggressively enforces the Protection Obligation (section 24) against data leaks, producing dozens of published decisions including against Marina Bay Sands. The Access Obligation (section 21) has produced none. Not one published decision, across over a decade of enforcement action. This is the Access Obligation that is supposed to give Singaporeans the right to request their own data from organisations. The site at [https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html) documents the full matrix, and I believe the pattern reflects a de facto policy of non-enforcement implemented through invented evidentiary standards.

You know my character and my track record. I served with integrity during Covid, received a Commendation Medal, and completed my MBA at SMU with a 4.0 GPA. I approach this matter with the same discipline: the documentary record is publicly available, and I stand ready to be corrected if any conclusion is wrong.

As a backbencher, what procedural options are available to you to raise a documented systemic pattern in Parliament, even when the Government side may not prioritise it?
