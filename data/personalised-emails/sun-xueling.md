**Subject: Follow-up on the PDPC grievance, an update and a request for your attention**

Dear Ms Sun Xueling,

As Senior Minister of State at MND and MOT, your portfolio intersects with the same public sector institutions that the documented PDPC pattern implicates. I am writing to share three findings and to ask what you can do within your remit to help address this.

PDPC recently redesigned its enforcement-decisions page so that it is no longer possible to filter published cases by obligation type. The previous filter exposed a striking pattern: across its entire published enforcement record, PDPC has never once found a breach of the Access Obligation. The new layout hides that pattern entirely. Anyone who previously relied on the filter to assess PDPC's enforcement record can no longer do so.

To use a sporting analogy: when a referee is also the governing body, and a player scores a goal by handling the ball, the referee simply declines to look. VAR exists today, and still the referee looked away. PDPC sits in judgement of its own decisions. Nothing happens. The enforcer investigates itself and finds nothing to enforce.

The numbers bear this out. Across PDPC's entire published enforcement record, documented at https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html, there has not been a single breach finding under the Access Obligation, even as PDPC has aggressively pursued data-breach cases under the Protection Obligation. This asymmetry between the two enforcement tracks is itself a documented pattern. In my own case, the CCTV footage that would have shown what happened to me was denied access to, and was subsequently deleted, while my statutory access request was still pending and active.

Would you consider raising this through your channels, so that this pattern can receive the parliamentary scrutiny it warrants?

Three sites to verify:
- https://pdpaaccessrights.sg
- https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html
- https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/organisations/regulations-decisions/enforcement-decisions?type=Commission%27s+Decisions&page=1&sort=latest

Yours sincerely,
Lim Zirui (Ray Lim)
