Dear Ms He Ting Ru,

As one of the newer voices representing Sengkang GRC, you have an opportunity to engage with issues that affect your residents in ways that may not have received parliamentary attention yet. The issue I want to raise falls into that category, and I write in the hope that you might find it worthy of examination.

In March 2025, PDPC removed a filter from its published enforcement-decisions page. That filter had enabled public segmentation of cases by obligation type. After the redesign, the segmentation is gone, and the zero Access Obligation breach finding, visible in the data before the change, is no longer accessible to a regular user without specialised knowledge of the site. The information was made inconvenient, not removed.

The Maradona Hand-of-God goal is the most honest shorthand I have found for what PDPC has done. The referee was asked to review the play. He declined. Decades passed, VAR arrived, and the referee still declined to use it. The enforcer who declines to examine the evidence lets the result stand. Nothing compels a different outcome. That is the dynamic I am asking you to consider.

Section 21 of the Personal Data Protection Act gives individuals the right to access data an organisation holds about them. PDPC has never found a breach of that right in any of its 374 published enforcement decisions. In contrast, it actively pursues Protection Obligation cases under s.24, obtaining fines and compliance requirements. The asymmetry is documented at [https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html.](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html.)

Mr Lim, I have rebuilt the filter independently at [https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html) so that this data remains publicly accessible. Every Singaporean deserves to know that the Access Obligation, a right written into law, has never produced a published enforcement outcome in the history of the PDPA.

Would you consider a substantive motion or parliamentary question on behalf of residents who have found this right unenforceable in practice?

Three sites to verify:
- [pdpaaccessrights.sg](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg) — full account of the documented pattern, the rebuilt enforcement filter, and the primary record
- [pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html) — the rebuilt enforcement matrix showing 0 Access Obligation breach findings across 374 published decisions
- [pdpc.gov.sg/enforcement-decisions](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/organisations/regulations-decisions/enforcement-decisions?type=Commission%27s+Decisions&page=1&sort=latest) — the official PDPC published-decisions page, filterable by obligation type
