Dear Mr Low Wu Yang Andre,

Your role as a Non-Constituency Member of Parliament, combined with your background in digital and youth-oriented spaces, positions you well to engage with an issue that sits at the intersection of data rights and digital access. I write in the hope that this falls within the scope of matters you are willing to champion.

In March 2025, PDPC removed a filter from its published enforcement-decisions website. The filter had enabled public segmentation of cases by obligation type. After the redesign, the segmentation is gone, and the zero Access Obligation breach finding, which had been visible in the data before the change, became inaccessible to most members of the public. The information was made inconvenient, not erased.

The Maradona Hand-of-God goal is the analogy I keep returning to. The referee was asked to look at the evidence. He declined. VAR was introduced, and still he declined. When the enforcer declines to examine the record, nothing compels a different outcome. The result stands by inaction.

Section 21 of the Personal Data Protection Act gives individuals the right to access their personal data. Section 24 imposes a protection obligation on organisations. PDPC enforces s.24 actively, obtaining fines from organisations that fail to protect data. It has never published a breach finding under s.21 across 374 cases. The enforcement-index at [https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html) documents every case; the zero in the Access Obligation column is the only consistent finding.

Mr Lim, I have rebuilt the filter independently at [https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html) so that this data remains publicly accessible. Every Singaporean deserves to know that the Access Obligation, a right written into law, has never produced a published enforcement outcome in the history of the PDPA. The NCMP channel may offer a useful route to put this structural gap on the parliamentary record.

Would you consider raising this through NCMP channels?

Four sites to verify:
- [pdpaaccessrights.sg](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg) — full account of the documented pattern, the rebuilt enforcement filter, and the primary record
- [pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html) — the rebuilt enforcement matrix showing 0 Access Obligation breach findings across 374 published decisions
- [pdpc.gov.sg/enforcement-decisions](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/organisations/regulations-decisions/enforcement-decisions?type=Commission%27s+Decisions&page=1&sort=latest) — the official PDPC published-decisions page, filterable by obligation type
- [Hansard: Written Answer 19596](https://sprs.parl.gov.sg/search/#/sprs3topic?reportid=written-answer-19596) — Minister Josephine Teo's Parliamentary assurance of 22 September 2025 on data preservation + criminal penalties for intentional concealment
