**Subject: Follow-up on the PDPC grievance, an update and a request for your attention**

Dear Minister Masagos Zulkifli,

As Minister for Social and Family Development, your portfolio oversees some of the most vulnerable members of society, including children and families in contact with social service agencies, and individuals receiving government support. When the statutory right of access under the PDPA is not effectively enforced, it is these communities who are least equipped to challenge refusals and most harmed when evidence they are entitled to access is denied or destroyed. I am writing to share three findings and to ask what you can do within your remit.

PDPC recently redesigned its enforcement-decisions page so that it is no longer possible to filter published cases by obligation type. The previous filter exposed a striking pattern: across its entire published enforcement record, PDPC has never once found a breach of the Access Obligation. The new layout hides that pattern entirely.

To use a sporting analogy: when a referee is also the governing body, and a player scores a goal by handling the ball, the referee simply declines to look. VAR exists today, and still the referee looked away. PDPC sits in judgement of its own decisions. Nothing happens.

The numbers bear this out. Across PDPC's entire published enforcement record, documented at [https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html,](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html,) there has not been a single breach finding under the Access Obligation, even as PDPC has aggressively pursued data-breach cases under the Protection Obligation. In my own case, CCTV footage that would have shown what happened to me was denied and then deleted while my access request was still live.

Would you consider reviewing whether the social and family service agencies under your charge have the systems and oversight needed to honour the statutory right of access that every vulnerable individual holds under the PDPA?

Four sites to verify:
- [pdpaaccessrights.sg](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg) — full account of the documented pattern, the rebuilt enforcement filter, and the primary record
- [pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html](https://pdpaaccessrights.sg/enforcement-index.html) — the rebuilt enforcement matrix showing 0 Access Obligation breach findings across 374 published decisions
- [pdpc.gov.sg/enforcement-decisions](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/organisations/regulations-decisions/enforcement-decisions?type=Commission%27s+Decisions&page=1&sort=latest) — the official PDPC published-decisions page, filterable by obligation type
- [Hansard: Written Answer 19596](https://sprs.parl.gov.sg/search/#/sprs3topic?reportid=written-answer-19596) — Minister Josephine Teo's Parliamentary assurance of 22 September 2025 on data preservation + criminal penalties for intentional concealment
