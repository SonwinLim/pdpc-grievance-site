Voluntary Undertaking by Rotary Engineering Pte Ltd

Published on 07 May 2026

Background
Rotary Engineering Pte. Ltd. (the "Organisation") notified the Personal Data Protection Commission (the "Commission") on 3 June 2025 of a ransomware attack on its corporate systems (the "Incident").
Investigations revealed that the threat actor had gained unauthorised access to the Organisation's corporate systems which resulted in ransomware deployment and exfiltration of personal data affecting 2,209 individuals (current and ex-employees of the Organisation).
The types of personal data affected included a combination of names, addresses, dates of births, signatures, image copies of passports, NRICs and birth certificates, resumes, pay slips and IR8A.
Upon discovery of the Incident, the Organisation took prompt remedial actions including disabling legacy authentication, implementing multi-factor authentication ("MFA") across network and VPN, applying conditional access controls and token revocations, disabling remote management for all devices, deploying a new "Green" environment that consists of a new network, rebuilt servers and rebuilt endpoint with no overlap with the original, compromised environment. The Organisation also notified the affected individuals.
